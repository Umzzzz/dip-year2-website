Judo

Judo is a remarkable and vibrant combat sport that demands both physical ability and great mental discipline. Judo starts from a standing position and involves specific techniques that allow an individual to lift and throw their challengers on the floor. Judo also involves techniques that allow you to put your challengers by submission, by pinning, choke holding and joint locking them until they give up.

Judo started many years ago in Japan as a mixture of martial arts which were formed together and it was then developed further and used by samurai and feudal warrior class over hundreds of years ago. Judo was first designed to kill your challengers in a battle however later the techniques got developed so that individuals are able to practice without hurting their challengers.

Judo has simplified in the modern day as now it just requires two people to take part and use their body force of balance, power and movement to try to overcome the other individual. Judo has two sides to it, it can be very basic yet it can also become more advanced the further into the sport you go. You need to master the basic judo techniques and methods in order to advance further, including putting both your time, effort and energy into the sport.

Coach Profile

David Groom-Judo Black Belt

From a young age David has taken part in judo, and has now been in the sport for over 17 years. David’s greatest achievement has been representing Team GB for 13 years and this has led to him competing around the globe in various competitions. The black belt judo trainer has represented Great Britain at four European Championships, and a World Championships; winning a Bronze medal at the 2004 European Cadet Championships. Currently David fights in the 90 kg category for Great Britain Men’s Squad and holds a BJA 3RD DAN black belt.

Some of David’s recent achievements include:

Dec 2013 – GOLD British Closed Championships -90kg

Oct 2013 – GOLD Welsh Open

Sept 2013 – 7th Serbia European Cup -90kg

Sept 2013 – GOLD Chilean Open -90kg

Aug 2013 – Gold Eastern Area Open -90kg

Aug 2013 – Gold Lancashire Closed -90kg and Open
